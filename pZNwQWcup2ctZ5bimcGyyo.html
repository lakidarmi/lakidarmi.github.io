8 Golden rules of kidney protection

8 Golden rules of kidney protection

Kidney disease is a silent killer that affects the quality of life. There are several simple ways to reduce the risk of developing kidney disease.

1. Be physical active.

Keeping fit helps reduce blood pressure and therefore lowers the risk of chronic kidney failure. The concept of "movement for kidney health" is a global collective March, which involves celebrities, professionals and public figures, moving around in public places, walking, running and Cycling.

2. Maintain regular blood sugar monitoring.

More than 50% of people with diabetes develop kidney damage. It is important for them to undergo regular examinations and check their kidney function.

Defeat kidney damage due to diabetes can be reduced or even prevented if detected at an early stage. It is important to monitor your blood sugar level and regularly undergo prevention and follow-up with a doctor.

3. Control your blood pressure.

While many people know that high blood pressure can this can lead to a stroke or heart attack, few people know that this is also the most common cause of kidney damage.

The normal blood PRESSURE level is 120/80. At AD 139/89, a person is considered to have hypertension, and it is desirable to make lifestyle changes and adjust their diet. at the 140/90 level and above, you should discuss the condition with your doctor and regularly monitor your blood PRESSURE level. High blood pressure can cause kidney damage when combined with other factors such as diabetes, high cholesterol, and cardiovascular disease.

4. Eat natural foods and control your weight.

This can help prevent diabetes, heart disease, and other conditions associated with chronic kidney failure.

5. Reduce your salt intake.

The recommended sodium intake is 5-6 g of salt per day (about one teaspoon). To reduce your salt intake, try to limit the amount of processed foods and do not add salt to your diet. This will be easier to achieve if you prepare food with fresh ingredients.

6. Maintain adequate fluid intake.

It is assumed that to maintain good health it is recommended to drink from 1.5 to 2 liters of water per day, and during training - 3-4 liters per day.

According to researchers from Australia and Canada, drinking enough fluids helps the kidneys break down sodium, urea, and toxins in the body, which in turn leads to "significantly lower blood pressure." risk" of developing chronic kidney disease. Research results show that caution should be taken when excessive fluid intake, which can also lead to side effects. On the other hand, a small water intake may carry the risk of decreased renal function. It is important to remember that the correct answer is the level of fluid intake for any person depends on many factors, including gender, exercise, climate, sanitation, pregnancy and breast-feeding. People who have already had kidney stones are recommended to drink 2-3 liters of water a day to reduce the risk of new stones forming.

7. Not smoke.

Smoking slows blood flow to the kidneys. When less blood enters the kidneys, it impairs their ability to function normally. Smoking increases the risk of kidney cancer by about 50 percent.

8. Limit the use of over-the-counter tablets.

Conventional non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs anti-inflammatory aides such as ibuprofen and its like are known for their ability to cause kidney damage when taken regularly.

Such medications may not pose a significant risk if the kidneys are relatively healthy and helpers are used only in emergencies, but if if you have chronic pain, such as arthritis or back pain, consult your doctor to find a way to control the pain without risking kidney damage.

Check your kidney function if you have one or more "high risk" factors»:

- diabetes;

- hypertension;

- excess weight;

- a parent or other family member with kidney disease;

- if you are from Africa or Asia.
