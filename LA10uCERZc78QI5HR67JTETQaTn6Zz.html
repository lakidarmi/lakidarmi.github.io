Alcohol and sports

Alcohol and sports

It is no secret that the concepts of "alcohol" and" sport " are far from each other. The reason for this is the many risks associated with drinking alcohol both on and off the sports field. Many people believe that a couple of glasses of beer or guilt after a workout may not affect their fitness, but research in this area shows the opposite. However, despite all the known drawbacks, alcohol remains the most popular and may even be one of the oldest ways to relieve mental tension, relax the body, and boost energy self confidence.

Alcohol as doping in sports.

Alcohol, or its chemical name, ethanol, is on the world anti-doping Agency's (WADA) list of prohibited substances, and its use is prohibited for certain sports, during a game or competition. Alcohol slows you down brain activity does not lead to an increase in athletic performance, so it is not considered a substance that directly affects the improvement of performance. Alcohol is used as a doping agent to reduce anxiety, tremor of the hands when shooting, or to increase the sense of confidence in martial arts. due to the fact that alcohol consumption is associated with delayed reactions, it is prohibited in all types of motor sports.

Negative effects of alcohol on the human body.

The impact of alcohol on the human body is associated with both short-term and long-term negative effects. Short-term effects are associated with the negative effects of alcohol on the nervous system, metabolism and organ function. In the long run, damage is associated with fat accumulation, liver damage, and alcoholism.

Impaired concentration and reduced reaction time.

Alcohol consumption has an impact on depressing effect on the Central nervous system, leading to delayed reactions and impaired coordination. For this reason, alcohol consumption can become a prerequisite for serious sports injuries and situations that are dangerous to the life and health of athletes.

Dehydration.

Alcohol has a diuretic effect an effect that makes the kidneys work harder, and this leads to dehydration of the body. Good hydration of the body is a key factor for achieving good results in physical activity, so drinking alcohol can significantly reduce the strength and endurance of an athlete. Use alcohol affects your workout, even if it is scheduled for the next day, due to the fact that the body needs time to process alcohol and fully recover. Thus, post-alcohol recovery before physical activity or drinking large amounts of alcohol after physical activity can lead to significant dehydration, which is accompanied by many negative consequences for the body.

Load on the liver.

The liver plays a major role in the metabolism of alcohol, breaking it down into simpler compounds that are then used to generate energy. One of these compounds, acetaldehyde, it is a highly toxic substance that causes damage to liver protein and subsequent cirrhosis. The process of breaking down alcohol is related to its amount in the blood and individual metabolism, with the liver usually processing about 10-15 grams of alcohol in 1 hour. This process creates a significant load on the liver and disrupts the ability of the liver to function properly. its function, including the accumulation of glycogen and the release of glucose into the blood. Thus, in the absence of sufficient carbohydrates in the blood, strength and endurance during sports are reduced, and fatigue occurs much faster.

Decrease in the level of male hormone.

Research shows that alcohol consumption significantly reduces the level of male hormone in the blood and its synthesis. This affects the overall metabolism, preventing the use of fat for energy and anabolic processes in the muscles. For this reason, alcohol consumption can have a negative effect on maintaining muscle mass, which it is a particularly important element for achieving good sporting results.

Sleep disorders and inability to fully recover.

Although alcohol often leads to feelings of drowsiness,it does not help you sleep better and rest. With regular consumption, it interferes with the normal flow of sleep phases, and this means adequate rest and recovery of the body. Because of sleep disorders, alcohol prevents the formation of human growth hormone-somatotropin, which, like the male hormone, is very important for muscle growth and recovery after exercise.

Increased caloric intake and weight gain.

Although alcohol is not included in the group of essential nutrients, it contains 7 calories per gram. Unlike carbohydrates, it is toxic, cannot be stored in the body or used by the muscles for energy. When alcohol is consumed with high-calorie foods, its metabolism prevents the use of carbohydrates and fats in the diet. as a source of energy, which is why they accumulate in fat reserves. An increase in caloric intake and excess weight gain is a consequence of chronic alcohol abuse. An additional reason for increasing caloric intake is that alcohol significantly increases your appetite.
