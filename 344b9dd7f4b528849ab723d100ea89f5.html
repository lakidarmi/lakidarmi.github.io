Coronavirus and mental health: how to deal with the crisis?

When we face a crisis, it primarily affects our mental state negatively. Ironically, it is this experience that helps us survive the next one crisis.

When the brain is in crisis mode, it instinctively redirects the entire thought process to a direct response to the threat. If the threat is repeated periodically, it reduces the ability of the brain to adapt and recover, which affects the psyche. In the midst of THE current covid-19 crisis, adaptation and recovery is what is needed more than ever. Understanding that we are living in a crisis mode is the first step to restoring the balance of thinking and health.

The ability to counteract terrible thoughts, calmly and effectively act is very important for overcoming this crisis situation situations.

Coronavirus and mental health: how to deal with the crisis?

It is important to remember that there are things that can be controlled, and things that are completely out of control. Constant worry about these things causes an anxiety attack, a condition that directly affects the mental and physical health. Anxiety and stress also affect the immune system, which puts the body in a more vulnerable position when fighting an infectious disease.

The consequences of constant anxiety and stress greatly affect a person's ability to feel hopeful for both the immediate and long-term future. In many people there is a desolation caused by hopelessness in the eyes of relatives, friends and fellow citizens, as well as inhumane treatment and lack of empathy for those who struggle with mental or physical illness every day. The mood of people living with a particular mental disorder is very much affected by it symptoms.

The crisis situation makes it difficult to think normally, many people are forced to take medication and actively work to reduce anxiety and stress, and strive to contact other people in order to avoid complete isolation.

If you ask, is there a surefire way to completely eliminate mental anguish and stress? if there is no suffering, then the answer is that there is no such way. However, it can be said that there is a will in many different forms.

Humanity often shows willpower, as people strive to unite, trying to find creative ways to support each other, show empathy.

Developing skills that you can use emotions, such as empathy, significantly stabilizes the psyche and calms it down. Will can be defined as part of a person's character or personality. No one is born with a will, it needs to be "trained" like muscles.

There are 4 types of will, and just like every muscle, each of these types has a special purpose purpose:

1. the Will associated with attachment serves to create and maintain trusting relationships, to achieve a sense of connection with other people, and very good survival skills.

2. the Will associated with individual skills helps a person to become strong and successful, to get the support of their colleagues make a lot of effort and be inspired by good role models.

3. the Will to live helps you stay calm and find ways to deal with adversity or difficult situations. This also allows you to manage your own fears.

4. The will of the soul allows you to feel closer to nature and everyone people and gain additional strength and protection.

There are things that can be done daily to address extreme symptoms that affect mental health:

- try to be physically active, do stretches and exercises;

- if you are taking medication, take it as prescribed keep your prescription and make regular appointments with your doctor;

- eat normal healthy food and try to have a lot of fruits and vegetables on the menu every day;

- get your workplace in order and focus on your work, take small breaks to recover;

- try to go deep breathe and meditate;

- reduce your caffeine intake as it increases your anxiety level;

- conduct online and video conversations with friends and family;

- periodically take a break from social networks;

- see a therapist or psychologist if you are going through a very difficult time time;

- try to share positive news and opinions.
