Is the use of dried fruits useful?

Is the use of dried fruits useful?

Dried fruits help to diversify the diet, supplying the body with some important nutrients. Dried fruits contain a lot of fiber, minerals and b vitamins.

In process water vaporization from fruits reduces their size, but the nutrients in them are not lost, and become more concentrated. Half the dried fruit is equal to a whole plate of fresh ones. Before drying, the fruit is processed to preserve its natural color and prevent the multiplication of bacteria. This process is performed with natural substances, such as lemon juice, and in some cases, sulfur is used. Therefore, dried fruits are not recommended for people with asthma.

It should be borne in mind that dried fruits contain more carbohydrates and calories than fresh ones. You will have to forget about them if you want to to lose weight. However, dried fruits provide a lot of energy, which is important for sports fans.

During fruit drying, water-soluble vitamins are lost, but the nutrients, on the contrary, become more concentrated. Half a bowl of dried fruit contains almost as much vitamin B as the entire bowl of fresh fruit fruits. But it should be remembered that when drying vitamins C and A are lost.

Dried fruits are especially rich in fiber, which is important for good digestion and intestinal function, but you also need to know the measure. If you overdo it, you may have digestive problems. Excessive consumption of dried fruits causes gas, bloating, diarrhea, or constipation. In order to avoid these unpleasant effects, it is better to eat them sparingly.

Dried fruits contain quite a lot of natural sugar-fructose, and some fruits may include extra sugar used to process them. That is why you should not eat them constantly and a lot, because in addition to a set of several kilograms, you can harm your teeth.

Below are some of the most common and frequently consumed dried fruits.

Raisin.

Raisins are dried grapes. It is high in fiber, potassium, and has a low glycemic index (GI), which means that when consumed, there is no sharp rise in blood sugar (USC) levels. According to scientists, raisins are able to:

• lower your blood PRESSURE;

• to improve control of USK;

• quickly achieve a sense of satiety;

• reduce the risk of developing diabetes and CVD.

Prune.

It is very satisfying, in it a lot of vitamin K, potassium and provitamin A. it Is a natural laxative. This effect is due to the increased presence of fiber and sorbitol in it. Prunes are recommended for people suffering from chronic constipation.

In addition, dried fruit is high in antioxidants, it helps prevent some CVD and cancer.

Dates.

Dates are very sweet, rich in fiber, potassium and iron. Among dried fruits, they have the highest content of antioxidants and a low GI.
